Thank you for your detailed review. We appreciate the direct questions on formulation, realism, and framing. Below we provide one-to-one responses to each concern.

W1: The problem formulation is unclear.
A1: We agree that the formulation should have been stated earlier and more explicitly. The model input is: (i) the pre-stow slot-aligned mask state, (ii) the pre-stow RGB image, (iii) a new-item observation, and (iv) the stow intent. The prediction target is the post-stow slot-aligned mask state. We predict structured object-level layouts rather than final RGB images because the downstream signals we study, such as DLO and multi-stow state propagation, depend primarily on occupancy and geometry. In the revision, we will move this problem statement earlier and make the train/test signals explicit before presenting the architecture.

W2: The “world model” characterization is questionable.
A2: We agree that our claim should be interpreted narrowly. FOREST is a transition model over a task-relevant object-centric state space X, not a full RGB environment simulator. In that restricted sense, it is a world model for bin-state transitions under sparse snapshot supervision. We will revise the language to avoid suggesting that we solve full visual environment modeling. The intended claim is structured future-state prediction for robotic stow, not complete scene simulation.

W3: The canonical new-item mask is privileged information derived from the post-stow image.
A3: This is an important point, and we agree it is a real limitation rather than something to argue away. The main paper already states that the canonical mask is a proof-of-concept proxy for the in-bin contact-surface view, used to isolate the post-stow prediction problem under an assumption that such a view is available. To quantify how much this assumption helps, we additionally tested a weaker proxy: recovering the in-bin contact-surface view from the induct-view image using SAM3D, then training/evaluating FOREST with this synthetic condition. Performance decreases, as expected, but remains above heuristics: direct insert N-IoU/O-IoU = 0.5290/0.8443 and sweep insert = 0.4784/0.6678. We therefore view the canonical-mask assumption as helpful but not the sole reason for FOREST’s gains. We also agree that this does not close the deployment gap.

W4: The model may only be learning a placement transform for a known silhouette.
A4: We believe the results indicate behavior beyond rigid placement. If the task were only to place a known silhouette, one would expect limited improvement on the motion of pre-existing items. However, in sweep insert, O-IoU improves from 0.5287 for copy-paste(+gravity) to 0.6878/0.6906 for FOREST, suggesting better prediction of rearrangement of existing items when space is created. This is also consistent with the downstream evaluations: the DLO predictor driven by FOREST masks is much closer to the predictor driven by ground-truth masks than the heuristic alternatives are.

W5: Baselines are weak and there is no strong learned comparison.
A5: We agree. As Reviewer oQeb also notes, stronger learned baselines would make the evaluation more convincing. Our current comparison is limited to static heuristics because this exact setting is unusual: sparse pre/post supervision, object-centric mask states, intent conditioning, and real warehouse data. Since no prior learned baseline directly matches this setup, we complemented direct evaluation with downstream tasks. Still, we do not claim that the current experiments settle the comparison against all learned alternatives.

W6/Q1/Q2/Q3: Practical deployment remains unclear if the post-stow image is unavailable.
A6: We agree that a deployed system cannot use signals derived from the true future state. Our intended deployment story is therefore not “use the ground-truth post-stow mask,” but “treat the canonical mask as a proxy for a contact-surface view that would need to be obtained before execution,” e.g., by a separate perception module. The additional SAM3D experiment above is meant to partially quantify this gap using a weaker pre-insertion proxy from the induct-view image. We will make this distinction more explicit so that the rebuttal does not overstate deployment readiness.
